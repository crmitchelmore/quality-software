# Customer Development

> Replace product-plan execution with systematic customer discovery, validation, creation and company-building, proving that a market and repeatable sales process exist before scaling.

**Discipline:** product · **Origin:** Steve Blank · *The Four Steps to the Epiphany* · (2005)

**Also known as:** Four Steps to the Epiphany, CustDev

## Description

Customer Development is Steve Blank's method for startups operating under extreme uncertainty. Its central claim is that startups are not small versions of large companies: they are temporary organisations searching for a repeatable, scalable business model. Instead of writing a business plan and executing it internally, founders must get out of the building, test hypotheses with prospects, and use evidence to decide whether to pivot or proceed. The four steps — customer discovery, customer validation, customer creation and company building — separate learning from scaling so teams do not prematurely optimise a product or go-to-market motion that customers do not value.

**In practice.** State the customer, problem, value proposition, channel and revenue hypotheses explicitly; interview and observe prospects; test buying intent with pilots, letters of intent or early sales; revise the model when evidence fails; and postpone heavy scaling until a repeatable acquisition and fulfilment pattern is visible.

## Core tenets

- Startups search for a repeatable and scalable business model; established companies execute one.
- Get out of the building and test assumptions with real customers, not internal opinions.
- Separate customer discovery and validation from demand creation and company scaling.
- Treat business-model components as hypotheses to be tested, revised or abandoned.
- Do not scale sales, marketing or hiring until the product and selling process have been validated.

## Key ideas

- **Four steps** — Customer discovery, customer validation, customer creation and company building form a staged learning process from problem evidence to scalable execution.
- **Customer validation** — Proof that a target segment will buy through a repeatable sales process, not merely that interviewees like the idea.
- **Pivot** — A substantive change to customer, problem, channel, pricing or product hypotheses when evidence contradicts the current model.

## Associated practice patterns

Product / UX patterns that embody or operationalise this philosophy:

- [Continuous Customer Interviewing](../product-patterns/continuous-interviewing.md) — Ongoing customer conversations operationalise Blank's instruction to get out of the building and replace assumptions with market evidence.
- [The Mom Test Interviewing](../product-patterns/the-mom-test.md) — Behaviour-focused interviewing helps avoid the false positives that Customer Development warns against.
- [Business Model Canvas](../product-patterns/business-model-canvas.md) — The canvas makes the business-model hypotheses explicit enough to test during discovery and validation.

## Criticisms / limits

- Customer interviews can still mislead if teams ask about opinions rather than behaviour or purchasing evidence.
- The method is less prescriptive for mature products where the business model is already known and optimisation is the main work.
- Founders sometimes over-index on what current prospects say and underweight strategic vision or latent demand.
