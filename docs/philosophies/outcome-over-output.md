# Outcome Over Output

> Manage product work by the customer and business behaviour changes it creates, not by the volume of features, tickets or roadmap items delivered.

**Discipline:** product · **Origin:** Josh Seiden, Marty Cagan · *Outcomes Over Output* · (2019)

**Also known as:** Outcomes Over Output, Outcome-Based Product Management

## Description

Outcome Over Output argues that teams should be accountable for meaningful changes in behaviour and business results rather than for shipping pre-specified features. Josh Seiden frames outcomes as changes in human behaviour that drive business impact, while Marty Cagan's outcome-based team model gives empowered teams problems to solve instead of feature lists to implement. The philosophy does not reject delivery; it insists that delivery is a means to an end and that roadmaps, discovery and experiments should connect work to measurable customer or business outcomes.

**In practice.** Translate strategy into product outcomes; define leading indicators of changed behaviour; create roadmap themes around opportunities; run discovery to find solutions; ship iteratively; and review whether shipped work moved the outcome instead of celebrating release completion alone.

## Core tenets

- Shipping features is not success unless it changes behaviour or improves a meaningful result.
- Product teams should receive problems and outcome goals, not merely feature mandates.
- Outcomes should be observable, measurable and connected to strategy.
- Roadmaps are better expressed as bets and outcome themes than fixed output commitments.
- Discovery and delivery should continually test whether outputs are actually producing the intended outcome.

## Key ideas

- **Outcome** — A measurable change in customer, user or stakeholder behaviour that contributes to a business result.
- **Output** — The artefact delivered — a feature, document, campaign or release — which has value only if it helps produce the outcome.
- **Outcome-based roadmap** — A roadmap organised around desired results and strategic bets rather than a calendar of committed features.

## Criticisms / limits

- Outcome goals can become vague slogans if teams do not define observable behaviour and leading indicators.
- Some outputs are mandatory for legal, platform or operational reasons even when their behavioural outcome is indirect.
- Senior stakeholders may still demand date and scope certainty, creating tension with outcome-based planning.
