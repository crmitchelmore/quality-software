# Empowered Product Teams

> Give durable cross-functional product teams meaningful problems to solve, strategic context and accountability for outcomes, rather than treating them as feature delivery squads.

**Discipline:** product · **Origin:** Marty Cagan, Chris Jones · *EMPOWERED* · (2020)

**Also known as:** Empowered Teams, Product Model

## Description

Empowered Product Teams is the philosophy promoted by Marty Cagan and Chris Jones for strong technology product organisations. It contrasts the product model with the feature-team model: leaders provide vision, strategy, context and coaching, while product teams discover and deliver solutions that achieve outcomes. A team is composed of product management, design and engineering, has durable ownership of a product area, and is trusted to make product decisions within strategic guardrails. Empowerment is paired with competence and accountability; it is not autonomy without direction.

**In practice.** Form durable teams around product areas; give each team outcome goals and strategic context; ensure product, design and engineering work together in discovery; maintain high standards for product managers and leaders; and review teams by customer and business impact rather than feature throughput alone.

## Core tenets

- Teams should be assigned problems and outcomes, not handed feature lists to build.
- Product, design and engineering must collaborate as peers from discovery through delivery.
- Leaders set vision, strategy, principles and context, then coach teams rather than micromanaging solutions.
- Durable ownership builds product knowledge and accountability better than temporary project staffing.
- Empowerment requires capable people, clear strategic guardrails and responsibility for results.

## Key ideas

- **Product team** — A durable, cross-functional group with the skills and authority to discover, build and improve a product area.
- **Product model** — An operating model where teams solve customer and business problems through discovery and delivery, replacing project-oriented feature factories.
- **Strategic context** — Vision, strategy, principles and business constraints that let teams make local decisions aligned with company direction.

## Criticisms / limits

- Empowerment rhetoric can mask unclear strategy if leaders fail to provide context and hard choices.
- The model depends on highly capable product managers, designers, engineers and leaders that organisations may not yet have.
- Some regulated or contract-driven environments constrain team autonomy more than the model assumes.
