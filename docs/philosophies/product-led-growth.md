# Product-Led Growth

> Make the product itself the primary driver of acquisition, activation, conversion and expansion by letting users experience value before or alongside sales contact.

**Discipline:** product · **Origin:** Wes Bush, OpenView Partners · *Product-Led Growth* · (2019)

**Also known as:** PLG

## Description

Product-Led Growth is a go-to-market philosophy in which the product becomes the main channel for growth. OpenView popularised and coined the term, and Wes Bush codified the approach for SaaS companies. Instead of relying only on sales-led qualification and demos, PLG reduces time to value through self-serve onboarding, freemium or free-trial access, activation loops, usage-based signals and in-product expansion paths. The product is not merely what is sold; it performs marketing, sales, customer success and retention work by helping users reach an aha moment and then broadening adoption.

**In practice.** Offer a low-friction trial, free tier or interactive demo; instrument the activation funnel; identify the aha moment; remove onboarding friction; price around value and usage; trigger sales or success intervention from product-qualified signals; and design expansion loops into collaboration, templates or usage growth.

## Core tenets

- Let users experience product value quickly, ideally before a sales conversation is required.
- Optimise activation and time-to-value as core growth levers.
- Use product usage signals to guide conversion, expansion and customer-success action.
- Align pricing, packaging and onboarding with the value users realise in the product.
- Treat growth loops and retention as product design problems, not only marketing campaigns.

## Key ideas

- **Aha moment** — The early experience where a user understands and feels the product's core value, making continued use more likely.
- **Product-qualified lead** — A user or account whose in-product behaviour indicates readiness for sales, expansion or customer-success engagement.
- **Self-serve adoption** — Acquisition and activation paths designed so users can start, learn and reach value without bespoke human assistance.

## Criticisms / limits

- PLG is harder for complex enterprise products that require procurement, integration or high-touch change management.
- Free tiers and trials can attract low-intent users and increase support cost if packaging is poor.
- Product data can reveal what users do but not always why they do it.
