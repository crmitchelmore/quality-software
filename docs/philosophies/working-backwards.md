# Working Backwards

> Start from the future customer experience, express it as a press release and FAQ, and work backwards to decide whether and how the product should be built.

**Discipline:** product · **Origin:** Amazon, Colin Bryar, Bill Carr · *Working Backwards* · (2021)

**Also known as:** PR/FAQ, Amazon Working Backwards

## Description

Amazon's Working Backwards philosophy forces product thinking to begin with the customer and the finished experience rather than with internal technology, organisational convenience or incremental feature ideas. The canonical PR/FAQ method asks a team to write a future-facing press release that explains the customer problem, benefit and launch narrative, plus a frequently asked questions document that tests feasibility, economics, adoption and operational details. The artefact is debated before major investment so leaders can sharpen the idea, expose assumptions and align on what must be true for the product to deserve building.

**In practice.** Draft a future press release in customer language; add external and internal FAQs; review the narrative with decision-makers; revise until assumptions are explicit; connect open questions to research or experiments; and only then commit teams and investment to delivery.

## Core tenets

- Begin with the customer problem and desired future experience, not the available solution.
- Write the launch narrative before building so the value proposition is concrete and testable.
- Use FAQs to expose assumptions about feasibility, economics, operations and customer adoption.
- Prefer clear written narratives over slide decks for high-quality product decisions.
- Iterate the PR/FAQ until the customer value and business logic are compelling or abandon the idea.

## Key ideas

- **Press release** — A concise future announcement describing the customer, problem, solution, benefits and why the product matters.
- **FAQ** — A detailed companion document answering customer, business, technical and operational questions before commitment.
- **Narrative decision-making** — Written memos create shared context and expose reasoning more effectively than presentation fragments.

## Criticisms / limits

- A polished PR/FAQ can make an unvalidated idea feel more certain than it is.
- The method is writing-intensive and may slow smaller teams if used for every minor feature.
- It requires a culture willing to critique documents deeply rather than rubber-stamp narratives.
